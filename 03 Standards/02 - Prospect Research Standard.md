# Prospect Research Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the minimum research required before any outreach is initiated with a prospective client.

---

# Principle

Preparation creates better conversations.

Businesses respond more positively when they feel understood rather than targeted.

Research transforms cold outreach into relevant outreach.

---

# Why It Matters

Insufficient research leads to:

- Generic messaging
- Poor response rates
- Missed opportunities
- Lower credibility
- Longer sales cycles
- Weak discovery conversations

Effective research allows every outreach effort to begin with context instead of assumptions.

---

# Standard

Every qualified prospect must be researched before the first contact attempt.

Research should establish a working understanding of:

- The business
- Its products or services
- Target customers
- Geographic market
- Competitive position
- Current marketing activity
- Website quality
- Search visibility
- Advertising presence
- Reputation and reviews
- Growth opportunities

Research should identify both strengths and measurable opportunities for improvement.

The objective is not to know everything—it is to know enough to have an informed, valuable conversation.

---

# Decision Framework

Before outreach, ask:

Do we understand what this business does?

Can we explain how it makes money?

Have we identified measurable growth opportunities?

Can we reference something specific about the business?

Would our first conversation provide value instead of asking for it?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, continue researching before making contact.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Average research time per prospect
- Research completion rate
- Outreach response rate
- Discovery call conversion rate
- Opportunities identified per prospect
- Research accuracy
- Sales conversations influenced by research
- Close rate by research completeness

---

# Continuous Improvement

Research standards improve through:

- Successful sales conversations
- Lost opportunities
- Client onboarding insights
- New marketing tools
- Competitive analysis
- Emerging acquisition channels

Every completed engagement should improve future research quality.
