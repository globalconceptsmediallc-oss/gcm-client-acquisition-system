# Client Retention Handoff Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for transferring a newly acquired client from the Client Acquisition System to the Business Growth System. The handoff ensures continuity, preserves organizational knowledge, aligns expectations, and enables immediate execution without restarting discovery.

---

# Principle

Client acquisition is complete only when the client is successfully integrated into the Business Growth System.

A successful sale is measured by a successful transition.

Knowledge should never be lost during a handoff.

---

# Why It Matters

The handoff between acquisition and delivery is one of the highest-risk points in the client lifecycle.

Poor handoffs create:

- Misaligned expectations
- Lost information
- Delayed execution
- Duplicate work
- Reduced client confidence
- Lower retention
- Missed growth opportunities

A standardized handoff creates consistency, accountability, and momentum from day one.

---

# Standard

Every new client must complete a formal handoff before active implementation begins.

The handoff must transfer:

- Business understanding
- Client goals
- Agreed deliverables
- Strategic recommendations
- Known risks
- Decision history
- Communication preferences
- Success metrics
- Next actions

Implementation begins only after the required information has been transferred.

---

# Handoff Objectives

The handoff exists to ensure that:

- Nothing important is forgotten.
- The client never has to repeat information unnecessarily.
- Every team member understands the client's business.
- The Business Growth System begins with complete context.
- Momentum from the sales process continues into implementation.

---

# Required Handoff Information

Every handoff must include:

## Client Information

- Business name
- Primary contacts
- Contact information
- Decision makers
- Billing information
- Service locations

---

## Business Overview

Document:

- Industry
- Products or services
- Target customers
- Competitive position
- Growth objectives
- Current challenges
- Existing marketing assets

---

## Discovery Summary

Summarize:

- Problems identified
- Opportunities discovered
- Business priorities
- Success criteria
- Risks discussed
- Important client insights

The discovery process should never need to be repeated.

---

## Recommended Strategy

Document:

- Services approved
- Strategic priorities
- Implementation phases
- Expected outcomes
- Initial milestones
- Dependencies

Recommendations become operational plans.

---

## Scope of Work

Record:

- Approved deliverables
- Timeline
- Budget
- Responsibilities
- Client responsibilities
- Success measurements

The approved proposal becomes the operational baseline.

---

## Communication Preferences

Document:

- Preferred communication methods
- Primary contacts
- Meeting cadence
- Reporting frequency
- Escalation contacts
- Availability considerations

Communication standards continue uninterrupted after onboarding.

---

## Technical Access

Identify required access including:

- Website platforms
- Analytics
- Advertising platforms
- CRM systems
- Social media
- Email marketing
- Hosting
- Domain management
- Third-party integrations

Access requests should begin immediately after onboarding.

---

## Immediate Priorities

Identify:

- High-priority actions
- Time-sensitive tasks
- Known risks
- Quick wins
- Client expectations for the first 30 days

Early momentum builds long-term confidence.

---

# Handoff Meeting Standard

When appropriate, conduct a structured handoff meeting.

Objectives include:

- Review client objectives.
- Confirm implementation priorities.
- Clarify responsibilities.
- Review timelines.
- Answer internal questions.
- Verify documentation completeness.

The meeting confirms organizational alignment before execution begins.

---

# Documentation Standard

Every completed handoff must be recorded.

Documentation should include:

- Date completed
- Responsible team member
- Required documentation checklist
- Outstanding items
- Pending access requests
- Client readiness status
- Business Growth System entry confirmation

No client enters implementation without documented completion.

---

# Quality Verification

Before closing the acquisition process, verify:

- Discovery is complete.
- Proposal matches implementation.
- Expectations are documented.
- Responsibilities are assigned.
- Access requirements are identified.
- Initial priorities are defined.
- Success metrics are established.

Incomplete handoffs must be corrected before implementation proceeds.

---

# Decision Framework

Before transferring the client, ask:

1. Can implementation begin without additional discovery?
2. Does the implementation team understand the client's business?
3. Have expectations been clearly documented?
4. Is every critical decision recorded?
5. Can another qualified team member immediately continue the relationship?

If any answer is no, the handoff is incomplete.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Handoff completion rate
- Documentation completeness
- Time from signed agreement to implementation
- Client onboarding satisfaction
- Internal handoff errors
- Missing information incidents
- Implementation delays
- Client retention during the first 90 days

A high-quality handoff reduces implementation friction and improves long-term client success.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Improve the handoff process through:

- Onboarding reviews
- Client feedback

- Documentation audits
- Retention analysis
- Project retrospectives
- Business Growth System performance reviews

Every implementation challenge should be evaluated to determine whether improvements to the handoff standard are needed.
