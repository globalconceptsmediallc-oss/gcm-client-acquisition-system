# Prospecting Workflow Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard workflow for moving a qualified prospect from research to the first meaningful business conversation.

---

# Principle

Consistency produces predictable results.

Prospecting is not a collection of random activities. It is a repeatable system built on qualification, research, documented opportunities, and disciplined execution.

A standardized workflow ensures every prospect receives the same level of preparation, professionalism, and follow-through.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a defined workflow:

- Outreach becomes inconsistent.
- Opportunities are missed.
- Follow-up is forgotten.
- CRM data becomes unreliable.
- Performance is difficult to measure.
- Results depend on individual effort instead of a proven process.

A documented workflow creates repeatable execution that can be measured, improved, and scaled.

---

# Standard

Every prospect moves through the same acquisition workflow.

The minimum workflow is:

1. Confirm the business meets the Ideal Client Standard.
2. Complete Prospect Research.
3. Identify one or more measurable growth opportunities.
4. Record findings in the CRM.
5. Determine the most appropriate outreach strategy.
6. Initiate the first contact.
7. Record the outcome of every interaction.
8. Schedule the next action before closing the current one.
9. Continue follow-up until the prospect reaches a defined outcome.

Defined outcomes include:

- Discovery meeting scheduled
- Qualified opportunity
- Client acquired
- Nurture status
- Disqualified
- No response after standard follow-up sequence

No prospect should remain without a documented next step.

---

# Decision Framework

Before moving a prospect to the next stage, ask:

Has every previous workflow step been completed?

Is the CRM fully updated?

Is there a documented growth opportunity?

Is the next action clearly defined?

Would another team member understand exactly where this prospect stands?

If any answer is no, the workflow is incomplete.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Prospects entering the workflow
- Workflow completion rate
- First-contact rate
- Response rate
- Discovery meeting rate
- Follow-up completion rate
- Average days between stages
- Client acquisition rate
- Time from qualification to first conversation
- Time from first contact to signed client

---

# Continuous Improvement

The workflow should be reviewed whenever:

- Bottlenecks develop.
- Conversion rates decline.
- New acquisition channels are introduced.
- CRM improvements are identified.
- Automation opportunities become available.
- Team feedback identifies unnecessary complexity.

Every completed sales cycle should improve the efficiency of the workflow.
