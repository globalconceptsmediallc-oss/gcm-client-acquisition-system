# Opportunity Identification Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define how measurable business growth opportunities are identified before outreach begins.

---

# Principle

Businesses do not buy marketing.

They invest in solving business problems and creating measurable growth.

Every outreach effort should begin with an identifiable opportunity that can improve business performance.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a defined opportunity:

- Outreach becomes generic.
- Conversations lack relevance.
- Trust develops more slowly.
- Response rates decline.
- Sales become dependent on persuasion instead of value.

Opportunity-driven acquisition creates conversations that are based on observable business needs rather than assumptions.

---

# Standard

Every qualified prospect must have at least one documented growth opportunity before outreach begins.

Opportunities should be supported by objective evidence whenever possible.

Examples include:

- Technical SEO issues
- Missing or incomplete analytics
- Website performance problems
- Poor search visibility
- Weak local SEO
- Low review volume or poor reputation management
- Paid advertising inefficiencies
- Missing conversion tracking
- Weak calls-to-action
- Poor mobile usability
- Inconsistent branding
- Content gaps
- Broken customer journeys
- Underutilized digital assets
- Competitive disadvantages
- Market expansion opportunities

Each opportunity should be specific enough to explain during an initial conversation and substantial enough to create business value if addressed.

---

# Decision Framework

Before outreach, ask:

Have we identified a measurable opportunity?

Can we explain why it matters to the business?

Can the opportunity be supported with evidence?

Can we communicate the opportunity clearly without overwhelming the prospect?

Would solving this issue reasonably improve business performance?

If the answer to any question is no, continue research before initiating contact.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Opportunities identified per prospect
- Average opportunities discovered
- Opportunity-to-conversation rate
- Opportunity-to-discovery-call rate
- Opportunity-to-client conversion rate
- Most common opportunity categories
- Revenue generated by opportunity type
- Accuracy of opportunity assessments

---

# Continuous Improvement

Opportunity identification improves through:

- Sales conversations
- Client onboarding
- Campaign performance
- Technical audits
- Industry research
- Competitive analysis
- Emerging marketing technologies

New patterns discovered during client engagements should be incorporated into future opportunity assessments.
