# Recommendation Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard for developing and presenting recommendations that create measurable business growth for qualified prospects.

---

# Principle

Recommendations must solve business problems, not sell services.

Every recommendation should be based on research, discovery, measurable opportunities, and the prospect's business objectives.

The quality of the recommendation determines the quality of the client relationship.

---

# Why It Matters

Generic recommendations create:

- Lower trust
- Lower close rates
- Unrealistic expectations
- Poor client fit
- Scope confusion
- Reduced long-term results

Well-developed recommendations demonstrate expertise, establish credibility, and provide a clear path toward measurable business growth.

---

# Standard

Every recommendation must be developed using information gathered during:

- Prospect qualification
- Prospect research
- Opportunity identification
- Discovery conversations

Recommendations should:

- Address documented business challenges
- Prioritize measurable business outcomes
- Explain why each recommendation matters
- Describe expected business impact
- Set realistic expectations
- Align with the prospect's goals
- Support long-term partnership rather than short-term projects

Recommendations should never include services that do not directly contribute to measurable business growth.

---

# Decision Framework

Before presenting a recommendation, ask:

Does this solve a documented business problem?

Can the expected business impact be explained?

Is the recommendation supported by research?

Is it realistic to implement?

Does it align with the client's priorities?

Would we recommend this if we were investing our own money?

If any answer is no, revise the recommendation before presenting it.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Recommendations delivered
- Recommendation acceptance rate
- Proposal conversion rate
- Average implementation value
- Time from discovery to recommendation
- Client satisfaction with recommendations
- Revenue generated from recommendations
- Long-term client success

---

# Continuous Improvement

Recommendation quality should improve through:

- Client feedback
- Closed business reviews
- Lost proposal analysis
- Campaign performance
- Business outcome measurement
- Industry experience
- Emerging marketing strategies

Every implemented recommendation should improve future recommendation quality.
