# Follow-Up Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard for maintaining consistent, timely, and value-driven follow-up with qualified prospects throughout the client acquisition process.

---

# Principle

Persistence creates opportunity.

Most client relationships are not established during the first conversation. Consistent, professional follow-up demonstrates reliability, builds trust, and increases the likelihood of a successful long-term partnership.

Follow-up is a continuation of the relationship—not a repetition of the sales pitch.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a defined follow-up standard:

- Qualified opportunities are forgotten.
- Trust is weakened through inconsistency.
- Pipeline forecasts become unreliable.
- Response rates decline.
- Sales cycles become unpredictable.
- Revenue opportunities are unnecessarily lost.

A disciplined follow-up process ensures that every qualified prospect receives appropriate attention until a clear outcome is reached.

---

# Standard

Every qualified prospect must have a documented next action before the current interaction is considered complete.

Follow-up activities should:

- Be scheduled within the CRM.
- Reference previous conversations whenever possible.
- Deliver value rather than simply requesting a response.
- Reflect the prospect's current stage in the acquisition process.
- Continue until a defined disposition is reached.

A prospect remains active until one of the following outcomes is documented:

- Discovery meeting scheduled
- Proposal delivered
- Client acquired
- Nurture status assigned
- Prospect disqualified
- Prospect declines further communication
- Standard follow-up sequence completed with no response

No active prospect should exist without a scheduled follow-up or documented disposition.

---

# Decision Framework

Before closing any interaction, ask:

Has the next follow-up been scheduled?

Does the follow-up provide additional value?

Is the timing appropriate for the prospect's stage?

Is the CRM fully updated?

Has a clear objective been established for the next interaction?

If any answer is no, the follow-up process is incomplete.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Follow-up completion rate
- Average time between interactions
- Overdue follow-ups
- Response rate by follow-up attempt
- Discovery meetings generated through follow-up
- Opportunities reactivated
- Average sales cycle length
- Client acquisition rate by follow-up sequence
- Lost opportunities due to inactivity

---

# Continuous Improvement

The follow-up process should be reviewed whenever:

- Response rates decline.
- Opportunities stall.
- Sales cycles lengthen.
- New communication channels are introduced.
- CRM automation improves.
- Team feedback identifies better practices.

Every completed sales cycle should improve the timing, quality, and effectiveness of future follow-up activities.
