# Proposal Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard for preparing and presenting proposals that clearly communicate the scope, value, expectations, and next steps of a client partnership.

---

# Principle

A proposal should create clarity, not complexity.

The proposal is not intended to convince a prospect to buy. It documents the agreed-upon path for solving identified business problems and achieving measurable growth.

A well-structured proposal reduces uncertainty and establishes the foundation for a successful working relationship.

---

# Why It Matters

Poor proposals create:

- Misaligned expectations
- Scope confusion
- Pricing objections
- Delayed decisions
- Difficult onboarding
- Reduced trust

Clear proposals help both parties understand exactly what success looks like before work begins.

---

# Standard

Every proposal must be based on completed qualification, research, discovery, and recommendation activities.

A proposal should include, at a minimum:

- Executive summary
- Business objectives
- Documented opportunities
- Recommended strategy
- Scope of work
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Investment
- Success measurements
- Client responsibilities
- Agency responsibilities
- Next steps

Proposals should be written in plain language and focus on business outcomes rather than technical terminology whenever possible.

Every proposal should reflect the specific needs of the prospect rather than relying on generic templates.

---

# Decision Framework

Before delivering a proposal, ask:

Does the proposal address the documented business objectives?

Is the scope clearly defined?

Are responsibilities understood by both parties?

Can success be measured?

Does the investment align with the expected value?

Would another team member deliver this proposal exactly as written?

If any answer is no, revise the proposal before presentation.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Proposals delivered
- Proposal acceptance rate
- Average proposal value
- Time from discovery to proposal
- Time from proposal to decision
- Proposal revision rate
- Proposal-to-client conversion rate
- Revenue generated from accepted proposals

---

# Continuous Improvement

Proposal standards should improve through:

- Closed business reviews
- Lost proposal analysis
- Client feedback
- Onboarding outcomes
- Scope change requests
- Sales performance analysis
- Lessons learned from completed engagements

Every accepted or declined proposal provides information that should improve future proposals.
