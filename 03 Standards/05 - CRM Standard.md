# CRM Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard for recording, managing, and maintaining all prospect and client acquisition information within the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system.

---

# Principle

If it is not documented, it did not happen.

The CRM is the single source of truth for every prospect, conversation, opportunity, and client acquisition activity.

Organizational memory must exist independently of any individual team member.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a disciplined CRM standard:

- Prospect information becomes fragmented.
- Follow-up opportunities are lost.
- Conversations are repeated unnecessarily.
- Reporting becomes inaccurate.
- Team collaboration breaks down.
- Business development becomes dependent on individual memory.

A well-maintained CRM creates continuity, accountability, and measurable performance.

---

# Standard

Every qualified prospect must have a CRM record before outreach begins.

The CRM record should include, at a minimum:

- Business name
- Primary contact information
- Decision-maker(s), when known
- Industry and business category
- Geographic market
- Qualification status
- Research summary
- Documented growth opportunities
- Outreach history
- Discovery meeting notes
- Current pipeline stage
- Next scheduled action
- Activity timestamps
- Final disposition

Every interaction with a prospect must be recorded as soon as practical after it occurs.

No prospect should exist outside the CRM once they enter the acquisition system.

---

# Decision Framework

Before ending any prospect activity, ask:

Has this interaction been documented?

Is the current pipeline stage accurate?

Is the next action scheduled?

Would another team member understand the complete history by reviewing the CRM?

Can future reporting accurately reflect today's activity?

If any answer is no, the CRM record is incomplete.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- CRM record completion rate
- Required field completion percentage
- Activities logged
- Next actions scheduled
- Overdue follow-ups
- Pipeline accuracy
- Data quality audits
- Duplicate records
- Average response time
- Client acquisition rate by pipeline stage

---

# Continuous Improvement

The CRM standard should evolve through:

- Sales process reviews
- Pipeline analysis
- Team feedback
- Reporting improvements
- Automation opportunities
- New acquisition channels
- Lessons learned from successful and unsuccessful engagements

Changes should improve data quality, usability, reporting accuracy, and operational efficiency without adding unnecessary complexity.
