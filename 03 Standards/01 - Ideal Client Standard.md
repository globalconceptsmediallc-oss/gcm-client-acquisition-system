# Ideal Client Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:
Define the minimum requirements a business must meet before entering the Client Acquisition System.

---

# Principle

Not every business should become a prospect.

Time is the most limited resource in business development.

The highest-performing acquisition systems spend the majority of their effort pursuing organizations that can realistically become successful long-term clients.

Selection creates efficiency.

---

# Why It Matters

Poor client selection creates:

- Low close rates
- Poor client fit
- Difficult onboarding
- Scope creep
- Lower retention
- Reduced profitability
- Team frustration

Great acquisition starts before the first conversation.

The acquisition system succeeds because the right businesses enter it.

---

# Standard

Every prospect must be evaluated before entering the active acquisition pipeline.

Minimum evaluation areas include:

• Business legitimacy

• Market demand

• Ability to purchase services

• Growth opportunity

• Decision-maker accessibility

• Long-term partnership potential

Businesses failing minimum standards remain outside the active system until conditions change.

No prospect enters the acquisition workflow simply because they responded.

Qualification precedes pursuit.

---

# Decision Framework

Before active outreach ask:

Does this business appear stable?

Can marketing realistically create measurable growth?

Would we want this company as a long-term client?

Can we identify the decision maker?

Is there evidence they invest in growth?

Would success with this client strengthen our portfolio?

If multiple answers are negative, the business should not enter active prospecting.
