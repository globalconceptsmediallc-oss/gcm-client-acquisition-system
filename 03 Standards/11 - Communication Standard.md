# Communication Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:
Establish the communication standards that govern every interaction with prospects before they become clients. Every conversation must increase clarity, trust, and forward progress while accurately representing Global Concepts Media.

---

# Principle

Communication is not the transfer of information.

Communication is the reduction of uncertainty.

Every message should leave the prospect with greater confidence than before the interaction.

---

# Why It Matters

Poor communication creates confusion.

Confusion creates hesitation.

Hesitation delays decisions.

Delayed decisions reduce growth opportunities for both the prospect and Global Concepts Media.

Consistent communication builds predictable relationships, increases trust, shortens sales cycles, and improves conversion rates.

Communication is therefore treated as a measurable business process rather than a personality trait.

---

# Standard

Every communication must:

- Be timely.
- Be accurate.
- Be professional.
- Be respectful.
- Be concise.
- Be valuable.
- Move the relationship forward.
- Reduce uncertainty.
- Create trust.
- End with a clear next step whenever appropriate.

Communication exists to help prospects make better business decisions.

---

# Communication Principles

## Listen Before Speaking

Understand before recommending.

Questions create better conversations than assumptions.

---

## Educate Before Selling

Teach first.

Recommendations become obvious when prospects understand the problem.

---

## Be Honest

Never exaggerate.

Never overpromise.

Never create false urgency.

Long-term trust is worth more than short-term revenue.

---

## Speak Business

Avoid unnecessary marketing jargon.

Use language the business owner understands.

Translate technical concepts into business outcomes.

---

## Every Interaction Has Value

Even if a prospect never becomes a client, every interaction should improve their understanding of their business.

Every conversation should leave them better informed.

---

# Communication Channels

Approved communication includes:

- Phone
- Email
- Video meetings
- Text messaging
- CRM messaging
- LinkedIn
- Approved social messaging
- In-person meetings

Regardless of channel, communication standards remain identical.

The medium never changes the standard.

---

# Response Standards

Initial prospect inquiries should receive a response as quickly as reasonably possible.

Every response should acknowledge the inquiry.

Every response should establish expectations.

Every response should define the next step.

If additional research is required, communicate that research is underway rather than allowing silence.

---

# Meeting Standards

Every scheduled meeting must have:

- A defined objective.
- An agenda.
- Preparation completed beforehand.
- Notes recorded afterward.
- Action items assigned.
- Follow-up scheduled.

Meetings without outcomes are considered incomplete.

---

# Email Standards

Every email should include:

- A clear subject.
- A professional greeting.
- Short readable paragraphs.
- Clear recommendations.
- A specific call to action.
- Professional closing information.

Emails should answer questions before they are asked.

---

# Phone Standards

Calls should:

- Begin by confirming available time.
- Establish the objective.
- Ask questions before giving recommendations.
- Confirm understanding.
- Summarize decisions.
- Define next actions.

Every completed call must be documented inside the CRM.

---

# Internal Communication

Internal communication should be documented whenever it influences:

- Prospect strategy
- Recommendations
- Pricing
- Scheduling
- Deliverables
- Follow-up
- Client expectations

Organizational memory depends on documented communication.

---

# Documentation Standard

Significant communications must be recorded in the CRM.

Documentation should include:

- Date
- Participants
- Summary
- Decisions
- Commitments
- Next actions
- Follow-up date

If communication is not documented, it cannot reliably influence future decisions.

---

# Decision Framework

When communicating, ask:

1. Does this reduce uncertainty?
2. Does this increase trust?
3. Does this move the relationship forward?
4. Does this create measurable value?
5. Would another team member fully understand the conversation from the documentation alone?

If any answer is no, improve the communication before sending.

---

# Measurement

Communication quality is evaluated by:

- Response time
- Follow-up completion rate
- Meeting attendance rate
- Proposal acceptance rate
- Client onboarding rate
- CRM documentation completeness
- Prospect satisfaction
- Conversion rate

These metrics should improve as communication standards mature.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Communication standards should evolve through:

- Sales reviews
- Lost opportunity analysis
- Client feedback
- CRM audits
- Email performance
- Call reviews
- Proposal performance
- Team experience

Every missed opportunity is communication data.
