# Client Onboarding Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Define the standard for transitioning a new client from the Client Acquisition System into the Business Growth System through a structured, consistent, and measurable onboarding process.

---

# Principle

A successful client relationship begins with a successful transition.

The quality of onboarding establishes expectations, builds confidence, and creates the operational foundation for long-term business growth.

Every client should experience the same professional, organized, and repeatable onboarding process.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a defined onboarding standard:

- Expectations become unclear.
- Information is lost during handoff.
- Work begins without complete context.
- Client confidence decreases.
- Project delays become more likely.
- Long-term relationships are weakened before meaningful work begins.

A standardized onboarding process ensures that every client starts with clarity, organization, and confidence.

---

# Standard

Every signed client must complete the onboarding process before implementation work begins.

The onboarding process should include, at a minimum:

- Signed agreement verification
- Initial payment confirmation, when applicable
- Client record verification within the CRM
- Internal project creation
- Assignment of responsibilities
- Collection of required business information
- Collection of required platform access
- Definition of communication expectations
- Review of business goals and success measurements
- Confirmation of initial project priorities
- Scheduling of the kickoff meeting
- Formal transition into the Business Growth System

No implementation work should begin until all required onboarding activities have been completed or formally approved for exception.

---

# Decision Framework

Before completing onboarding, ask:

Has the client provided all required information?

Have all necessary platform accesses been received?

Are business goals documented?

Are success measurements defined?

Have communication expectations been established?

Has responsibility for ongoing work been assigned?

Is the client ready to transition into execution?

If any answer is no, onboarding remains incomplete.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Onboarding completion rate
- Average onboarding time
- Time from signed agreement to kickoff
- Missing onboarding requirements
- Client onboarding satisfaction
- Onboarding delays
- First-project start time
- Early client retention
- Onboarding completion accuracy

---

# Continuous Improvement

The onboarding process should improve through:

- Client feedback
- Internal project reviews
- Team retrospectives
- Implementation performance
- Early client success metrics
- Operational bottleneck analysis
- Process automation opportunities

Every completed onboarding should strengthen the consistency, speed, and quality of future client transitions.
