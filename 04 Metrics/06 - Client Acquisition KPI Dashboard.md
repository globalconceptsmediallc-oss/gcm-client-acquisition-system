# Client Acquisition KPI Dashboard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard executive dashboard for monitoring the overall health, performance, and effectiveness of the Global Concepts Media Client Acquisition System. The dashboard consolidates key performance indicators into a single operational view that supports timely, evidence-based leadership decisions.

---

# Principle

Leadership should not search for information.

The system should present the information leadership needs to make informed decisions.

A dashboard exists to create clarity, not complexity.

---

# Why It Matters

Business growth depends on timely decisions.

Without a standardized dashboard:

- Critical trends are overlooked.
- Problems are identified too late.
- Opportunities are missed.
- Forecasts become unreliable.
- Leadership spends more time collecting information than improving the business.

A consistent dashboard enables proactive management instead of reactive problem solving.

---

# Standard

The Client Acquisition KPI Dashboard is the official executive summary of the Client Acquisition System.

It shall:

- Consolidate critical acquisition metrics.
- Present current performance.
- Highlight trends.
- Identify risks.
- Support forecasting.
- Guide operational priorities.

Every KPI displayed must directly support a business decision.

---

# Dashboard Objectives

The dashboard exists to answer the following questions:

1. Is the acquisition system healthy?
2. Is the pipeline growing?
3. Are prospects progressing?
4. Are conversions improving?
5. Is revenue on target?
6. Where are the greatest risks?
7. What requires immediate leadership attention?

The dashboard should communicate system health in minutes, not hours.

---

# Executive KPI Categories

## Pipeline Health

Display:

- Active prospects
- Qualified prospects
- Pipeline value
- Weighted pipeline value
- Average pipeline age
- Pipeline growth rate

Healthy pipelines produce predictable growth.

---

## Sales Activity

Display:

- Calls completed
- Emails sent
- Discovery meetings completed
- Follow-ups completed
- Referral requests
- New opportunities created

Daily execution drives future performance.

---

## Conversion Performance

Display:

- Qualification rate
- Response rate
- Discovery conversion
- Proposal conversion
- Proposal acceptance
- Client acquisition rate

Conversion identifies where improvement efforts should be focused.

---

## Revenue Performance

Display:

- Monthly recurring revenue won
- One-time project revenue
- Revenue forecast
- Revenue variance
- Average client value
- Revenue by service category

Revenue validates acquisition effectiveness.

---

## Client Quality

Display:

- Ideal client percentage
- Referral clients
- Strategic fit score
- First 90-day retention
- Client satisfaction after onboarding

The objective is profitable, long-term client relationships.

---

## Operational Performance

Display:

- CRM completion rate
- Follow-up completion rate
- Proposal turnaround time
- Communication response time
- Documentation quality
- Handoff completion rate

Operational discipline supports sustainable growth.

---

# KPI Status Indicators

Each KPI should include a defined status.

## On Target

Performance meets or exceeds the established standard.

No corrective action required.

---

## Watch

Performance is trending below expectations.

Monitor closely and identify contributing factors.

---

## Action Required

Performance has fallen below acceptable standards.

Corrective actions should be assigned immediately.

Dashboard visibility should drive operational accountability.

---

# Trend Analysis

Every KPI should display:

- Current value.
- Previous reporting period.
- Percentage change.
- Long-term trend.
- Direction of movement.

Trends are more valuable than isolated measurements.

---

# Dashboard Design Principles

The dashboard should be:

- Easy to read.
- Consistent.
- Accurate.
- Action-oriented.
- Visually organized.
- Updated regularly.

The purpose is decision support—not reporting for its own sake.

---

# Leadership Review

Leadership should review the dashboard to determine:

- What improved?
- What declined?
- What created the change?
- Which trends require attention?
- Which operational changes should be prioritized?

Every review should result in measurable actions.

---

# Data Sources

Approved dashboard data includes:

- CRM
- Sales activity logs
- Proposal records
- Revenue reports
- Client onboarding records
- Referral tracking
- Pipeline reporting

Manual estimates should be minimized whenever possible.

---

# Dashboard Review Cadence

## Daily

Operational activity.

---

## Weekly

Pipeline, conversions, and priorities.

---

## Monthly

Revenue, forecasting, and strategic performance.

---

## Quarterly

Long-term trends, operational improvements, and acquisition strategy.

Review frequency should match leadership decision frequency.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing the dashboard, ask:

1. Which KPIs improved?
2. Which KPIs declined?
3. Which KPI represents the greatest business risk?
4. Which KPI offers the greatest improvement opportunity?
5. What operational change should be implemented first?

Every dashboard review should produce documented actions.

A valuable dashboard improves business performance—not simply visibility.
