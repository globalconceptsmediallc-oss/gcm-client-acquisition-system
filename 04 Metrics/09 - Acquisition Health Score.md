# Acquisition Health Score

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for evaluating the overall health of the Global Concepts Media Client Acquisition System using a single composite measurement. The Acquisition Health Score provides leadership with a clear, objective assessment of whether the acquisition system is operating at its expected level of performance.

---

# Principle

No single metric defines the health of a business.

System health is determined by the combined performance of its critical components.

The Acquisition Health Score measures the effectiveness of the acquisition system as a whole—not individual activities.

---

# Why It Matters

Leadership requires more than individual KPIs.

Individual metrics identify isolated performance.

The Acquisition Health Score identifies overall system performance.

Without an overall health measurement:

- Small problems remain unnoticed until they become major issues.
- Leadership focuses on isolated metrics instead of the complete system.
- Priorities become reactive.
- Long-term trends become difficult to recognize.
- Improvement efforts become fragmented.

A single health score creates clarity while encouraging balanced operational excellence.

---

# Standard

The Acquisition Health Score shall be calculated using multiple categories representing the entire Client Acquisition System.

No single category should dominate the final score.

Balanced performance is preferred over exceptional performance in one area while other areas decline.

---

# Objectives

The Acquisition Health Score exists to:

- Evaluate overall acquisition health.
- Identify early warning signs.
- Support leadership decisions.
- Prioritize improvement efforts.
- Measure long-term operational maturity.
- Validate continuous improvement efforts.

The score provides a strategic view of system performance.

---

# Health Categories

The score should evaluate performance across the following categories:

## Prospect Generation

Measure:

- Qualified prospects created.
- Prospect quality.
- Research completion.
- Pipeline growth.

A healthy system continuously creates new opportunities.

---

## Pipeline Health

Measure:

- Active opportunities.
- Stage distribution.
- Pipeline aging.
- Pipeline velocity.
- Opportunity value.

A healthy pipeline demonstrates continuous movement.

---

## Sales Activity

Measure:

- Prospecting consistency.
- Discovery meetings.
- Follow-up completion.
- Communication responsiveness.
- Relationship development.

Consistent execution supports predictable growth.

---

## Conversion Performance

Measure:

- Stage conversion rates.
- Proposal acceptance.
- Client acquisition rate.
- Referral conversion.

Healthy conversion demonstrates effective communication and value creation.

---

## Revenue Performance

Measure:

- Forecast accuracy.
- Revenue won.
- Monthly recurring revenue growth.
- Average client value.
- Revenue diversification.

Revenue validates acquisition effectiveness.

---

## Operational Excellence

Measure:

- CRM completeness.
- Documentation quality.
- Proposal turnaround.
- Handoff completion.
- Process compliance.

Operational discipline enables scalability.

---

## Client Quality

Measure:

- Ideal Client percentage.
- Strategic fit.
- First 90-day retention.
- Referral generation.
- Client satisfaction after onboarding.

The goal is acquiring the right clients—not simply more clients.

---

# Scoring Methodology

Each category should receive a standardized score using objective business measurements.

Leadership may assign category weightings based on strategic priorities.

Weightings should remain consistent throughout the reporting period to preserve historical comparisons.

Changes to weighting should be documented and justified.

---

# Health Classifications

## Excellent

The acquisition system consistently exceeds expectations.

Continue optimization and innovation.

---

## Healthy

Performance meets operational expectations.

Continue monitoring and incremental improvement.

---

## Watch

Performance shows early signs of decline.

Investigate contributing factors.

Prioritize corrective action.

---

## Critical

System performance is below acceptable standards.

Immediate operational review is required.

Recovery actions should be assigned without delay.

---

# Trend Analysis

Review:

- Current Health Score.
- Previous reporting period.
- Long-term trend.
- Category changes.
- Improvement trajectory.

Trend direction is often more valuable than the current score alone.

---

# Risk Indicators

Review whether declining scores result from:

- Reduced prospect generation.
- Pipeline stagnation.
- Poor follow-up.
- Weak proposals.
- Low conversion.
- Revenue concentration.
- Capacity limitations.
- Documentation deficiencies.

The Health Score should encourage investigation—not assumptions.

---

# Leadership Responsibilities

Leadership should use the Health Score to:

- Allocate resources.
- Prioritize improvements.
- Identify coaching opportunities.
- Validate strategic decisions.
- Guide investment.
- Support business planning.

The score exists to improve decision quality.

---

# Review Frequency

## Weekly

Review category movement.

---

## Monthly

Review overall Health Score.

Analyze contributing factors.

---

## Quarterly

Evaluate long-term trends.

Adjust strategic priorities when necessary.

Health should improve steadily over time.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing the Acquisition Health Score, ask:

1. Which category improved the most?
2. Which category declined the most?
3. What caused the change?
4. Which improvement will produce the greatest increase in overall system health?
5. Are resources aligned with the areas requiring attention?

Every review should result in measurable operational improvements.
