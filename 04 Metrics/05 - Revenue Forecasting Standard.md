# Revenue Forecasting Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for forecasting future revenue generated through the Client Acquisition System. Revenue forecasts provide leadership with objective, evidence-based projections that support planning, resource allocation, and sustainable business growth.

---

# Principle

Revenue should be forecasted from evidence—not optimism.

Reliable forecasts are built from measurable pipeline data, historical conversion performance, and documented business activity.

Forecasting is a decision-making tool, not a prediction of certainty.

---

# Why It Matters

Without accurate forecasting:

- Hiring decisions become risky.
- Marketing investments become reactive.
- Cash flow planning becomes uncertain.
- Capacity planning becomes difficult.
- Business growth becomes unpredictable.

A disciplined forecasting process enables Global Concepts Media to make proactive business decisions with greater confidence.

---

# Standard

Revenue forecasts must be based on documented opportunities within the Client Acquisition System.

Forecasts shall never rely solely on assumptions, intuition, or best-case scenarios.

Every forecast must be supported by measurable evidence.

---

# Forecasting Objectives

The Revenue Forecasting Standard exists to:

- Estimate future revenue.
- Measure pipeline health.
- Support operational planning.
- Identify revenue risks.
- Allocate business resources.
- Establish realistic growth expectations.
- Improve leadership decision-making.

Forecasts should reduce uncertainty—not eliminate it.

---

# Forecast Inputs

Revenue forecasts should include:

- Active opportunities.
- Pipeline stage.
- Estimated deal value.
- Historical conversion rates.
- Expected close date.
- Client type.
- Service type.
- Revenue model (recurring or one-time).

Every forecast begins with accurate pipeline data.

---

# Forecast Categories

## Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

Forecast recurring revenue generated from:

- Marketing retainers.
- Ongoing SEO.
- Paid advertising management.
- Website maintenance.
- Strategic consulting.
- Other recurring services.

Recurring revenue provides long-term business stability.

---

## One-Time Revenue

Forecast revenue from:

- Website projects.
- SEO audits.
- Strategy engagements.
- Branding projects.
- Consulting sessions.
- Other project-based work.

One-time revenue supports growth but should not replace recurring revenue.

---

## Weighted Pipeline Revenue

Estimate expected revenue by applying probability to each active opportunity.

Example methodology:

- Discovery Completed
- Recommendation Presented
- Proposal Delivered
- Negotiation
- Verbal Agreement

Weighted forecasting provides a more realistic view than total pipeline value alone.

The exact probability model may evolve over time but must remain consistent during each reporting period.

---

# Forecast Accuracy

Measure:

- Forecasted revenue.
- Actual revenue.
- Forecast variance.
- Percentage accuracy.
- Forecast reliability over time.

Forecasts improve through continuous comparison with actual business results.

---

# Revenue Risk Analysis

Every forecast should identify:

- High-risk opportunities.
- Large revenue dependencies.
- Delayed decisions.
- Stagnant proposals.
- Capacity limitations.
- Seasonal trends.
- Client concentration risk.

Understanding risk is as important as estimating revenue.

---

# Capacity Planning

Revenue forecasts should support planning for:

- Staffing.
- Contractor utilization.
- Project scheduling.
- Technology investments.
- Marketing budgets.
- Business development priorities.

Forecasting aligns acquisition with operational capacity.

---

# Forecast Review Cadence

## Weekly

Review:

- Pipeline movement.
- Proposal status.
- New opportunities.
- Revenue changes.

---

## Monthly

Review:

- Forecast accuracy.
- Revenue won.
- Revenue lost.
- Pipeline growth.
- Capacity alignment.

---

## Quarterly

Review:

- Long-term revenue trends.
- Strategic growth objectives.
- Service mix.
- Forecast methodology.
- Business expansion opportunities.

Forecasts should become more reliable through repeated review.

---

# CRM Requirements

Every revenue opportunity must include:

- Estimated value.
- Revenue category.
- Current stage.
- Expected close date.
- Assigned owner.
- Probability (if applicable).
- Supporting notes.
- Last activity.

Forecast accuracy depends upon disciplined CRM management.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing forecasts, ask:

1. Is every forecast supported by documented evidence?
2. Which opportunities present the greatest revenue risk?
3. Which opportunities require leadership attention?
4. Does projected revenue align with operational capacity?
5. What actions will improve forecast accuracy?

Every forecast should produce operational decisions.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Monthly forecast accuracy.
- Quarterly forecast accuracy.
- Revenue variance.
- Weighted pipeline value.
- Forecast confidence.
- Win rate.
- Average deal size.
- Average sales cycle.
- Revenue by service category.
- Revenue by client type.

Forecasting should improve as acquisition maturity increases.

# Continuous Improvement

Improve forecasting through:

- Historical analysis.
- Pipeline reviews.
- Conversion analysis.
- CRM audits.
- Win/loss analysis.
- Capacity reviews.
- Leadership reviews.
- Forecast validation.

Every reporting cycle provides data that improves future forecasts.
