# Reporting Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for creating, delivering, reviewing, and maintaining reports generated by the Global Concepts Media Client Acquisition System. Reporting transforms operational data into actionable business intelligence that supports leadership, accountability, and continuous improvement.

---

# Principle

Reports exist to improve decisions.

A report that does not influence a decision has little business value.

The purpose of reporting is clarity—not information overload.

---

# Why It Matters

The Client Acquisition System continuously generates operational data.

Without standardized reporting:

- Leadership receives inconsistent information.
- Decisions rely on assumptions.
- Accountability decreases.
- Performance trends remain hidden.
- Improvement opportunities are missed.
- Forecast confidence declines.

Standardized reporting creates a shared understanding of acquisition performance across the organization.

---

# Standard

All acquisition reports shall be:

- Accurate.
- Timely.
- Objective.
- Consistent.
- Action-oriented.
- Supported by documented business activity.

Every report should help the organization make better business decisions.

---

# Reporting Objectives

Every report should answer one or more of the following questions:

1. What happened?
2. Why did it happen?
3. What does it mean?
4. What should we do next?
5. Who is responsible for taking action?

Reports should create direction—not confusion.

---

# Report Categories

## Operational Reports

Focus on daily execution.

Examples include:

- Sales activity.
- Pipeline movement.
- Follow-up completion.
- CRM quality.
- Discovery scheduling.

Operational reports support immediate management decisions.

---

## Performance Reports

Measure results.

Examples include:

- Conversion rates.
- Proposal performance.
- Revenue generated.
- Client acquisition.
- Referral performance.

Performance reports evaluate system effectiveness.

---

## Executive Reports

Provide leadership summaries.

Include:

- KPI Dashboard.
- Acquisition Health Score.
- Revenue forecast.
- Strategic risks.
- Growth trends.
- Quarterly summaries.

Executive reports support strategic planning.

---

## Exception Reports

Identify unusual conditions.

Examples include:

- Stagnant opportunities.
- Missed follow-ups.
- Declining conversion rates.
- Forecast variance.
- CRM compliance issues.
- Capacity constraints.

Exception reporting enables proactive intervention.

---

# Reporting Requirements

Every report should include:

- Report title.
- Reporting period.
- Data source.
- Report owner.
- Generation date.
- Key metrics.
- Trend analysis.
- Significant observations.
- Recommended actions.

Context is as important as data.

---

# Data Integrity Standard

Report data must originate from approved systems, including:

- CRM.
- Proposal records.
- Revenue reporting.
- Client onboarding documentation.
- Sales activity logs.
- KPI Dashboard.

Manual estimates should be clearly identified.

Reliable reports require reliable data.

---

# Visualization Standard

Reports should emphasize clarity.

Use visual elements only when they improve understanding.

Examples include:

- Trend lines.
- KPI summaries.
- Pipeline stage visualization.
- Revenue comparisons.
- Conversion funnels.

Visual complexity should never replace business insight.

---

# Report Distribution

Reports should be delivered to the appropriate audience.

Examples include:

Leadership

- Executive summaries.
- Revenue forecasts.
- Quarterly reviews.

Operations

- Pipeline reports.
- Activity reports.
- CRM reports.

Business Development

- Prospecting reports.
- Conversion reports.
- Referral reports.

Each audience should receive information relevant to its responsibilities.

# Action Standard

Every report should produce one or more documented actions.

Actions should include:

- Description.
- Responsible owner.
- Due date.
- Expected outcome.
- Follow-up review.

Reporting without action creates little organizational value.

---

# Documentation Standard

Maintain records of:

- Reports generated.
- Distribution.
- Decisions made.
- Action items.
- Completion status.
- Historical performance.

Historical reporting creates organizational memory.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing any report, ask:

1. What is the most important insight?
2. What changed since the previous report?
3. Why did it change?
4. What action should be taken?
5. How will success be measured?

Reports should always lead to better decisions.

---

# Measurement

Evaluate reporting quality by measuring:

- Report accuracy.
- Timeliness.
- Leadership utilization.
- Action completion.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Decision quality.
- Operational improvements.
- Business growth outcomes.

The effectiveness of reporting is measured by the quality of decisions it enables.

---

- Leadership feedback.

- Dashboard improvements.
- CRM enhancements.
- Automation.
- Business Growth System alignment.
- Reporting audits.

Reporting should evolve alongside the Client Acquisition System.
