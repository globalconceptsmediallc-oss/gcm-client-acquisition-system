# Client Acquisition Scorecard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard scorecard used to evaluate the overall performance of the Client Acquisition System. The scorecard provides a single source of truth for measuring pipeline health, sales effectiveness, conversion performance, and continuous improvement.

---

# Principle

What gets measured can be improved.

What is not measured cannot be managed.

The purpose of measurement is not to judge performance—it is to improve the system.

---

# Why It Matters

Client acquisition is a business system.

Business systems require measurable outputs.

Without consistent measurement:

- Pipeline problems remain hidden.
- Forecasts become unreliable.
- Sales activity becomes inconsistent.
- Improvement becomes subjective.
- Growth becomes unpredictable.

A standardized scorecard transforms daily activity into actionable business intelligence.

---

# Standard

The Client Acquisition Scorecard is the primary measurement tool for evaluating acquisition performance.

It must be:

- Accurate
- Current
- Objective
- Repeatable
- Actionable

The scorecard should be reviewed regularly and used to guide operational decisions.

---

# Scorecard Objectives

The scorecard exists to answer five questions:

1. Are we creating enough opportunities?
2. Are we converting opportunities efficiently?
3. Are we acquiring profitable clients?
4. Is the acquisition pipeline healthy?
5. What should be improved next?

Every metric should help answer one or more of these questions.

---

# Core Performance Categories

## Prospect Generation

Measure:

- New prospects identified
- Qualified prospects
- Research completed
- Outreach opportunities created

Healthy acquisition begins with a consistent flow of qualified prospects.

---

## Prospecting Activity

Measure:

- Calls completed
- Emails sent
- LinkedIn outreach
- Meetings scheduled
- Networking activity
- Referral requests

Activity creates opportunities.

Consistency creates predictability.

---

## Pipeline Health

Measure:

- Active opportunities
- Discovery calls scheduled
- Proposals outstanding
- Average pipeline age
- Opportunities by stage
- Pipeline value

A healthy pipeline contains opportunities at every stage.

---

## Conversion Performance

Measure:

- Discovery-to-proposal conversion
- Proposal acceptance rate
- Prospect-to-client conversion
- Referral conversion
- Average sales cycle length

Conversion identifies where the acquisition system is strongest and weakest.

---

## Revenue Performance

Measure:

- New monthly recurring revenue
- One-time project revenue
- Average client value
- Revenue forecast
- Revenue won
- Revenue lost

Revenue validates acquisition effectiveness.

---

## Client Quality

Measure:

- Ideal client percentage
- Client retention after onboarding
- First 90-day satisfaction
- Growth potential
- Strategic fit

The objective is not simply to acquire more clients.

The objective is to acquire the right clients.

---

## Operational Excellence

Measure:

- CRM completeness
- Follow-up completion
- Communication response time
- Proposal turnaround time
- Documentation quality

Operational consistency produces scalable growth.

---

# Scorecard Review Process

Every review should answer:

- What improved?
- What declined?
- Why did it change?
- What action should be taken?
- Who owns the improvement?

Measurements without decisions provide little value.

---

# Data Integrity Standard

All scorecard data must originate from documented business activity.

Approved sources include:

- CRM
- Proposal records
- Meeting documentation
- Communication logs
- Revenue reporting
- Client onboarding records

Estimated values should be clearly identified.

Accuracy is more valuable than volume.

---

# Reporting Frequency

Recommended review cadence:

## Daily

- Activity metrics
- Follow-up completion
- New opportunities

---

## Weekly

- Pipeline health
- Conversion progress
- Revenue created
- Proposal status

---

## Monthly

- Acquisition trends
- Client quality
- Revenue performance
- Forecast accuracy

---

## Quarterly

- Strategic review
- Process improvements
- Long-term trends
- System optimization

Measurement frequency should match decision frequency.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing the scorecard, ask:

1. Which metrics improved?
2. Which metrics declined?
3. What caused the change?
4. Is this a system issue or an execution issue?
5. What single improvement will create the greatest business impact?

Every review should produce at least one measurable improvement initiative.

---

# Measurement

The scorecard itself should be evaluated by:

- Data accuracy
- Reporting consistency
- Decision usefulness
- Forecast reliability
- Operational adoption
- Improvement implementation
- Business growth outcomes

A useful scorecard drives better decisions.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Improve the scorecard through:

- Leadership reviews
- Sales feedback
- CRM audits
- Reporting analysis
- Metric relevance reviews
- Forecast validation
- Business Growth System alignment

As the Client Acquisition System evolves, the scorecard should evolve with it.
