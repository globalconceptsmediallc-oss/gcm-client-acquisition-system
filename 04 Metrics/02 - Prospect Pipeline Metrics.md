# Prospect Pipeline Metrics

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for measuring the health, performance, and progression of the prospect pipeline. The pipeline is the engine of the Client Acquisition System and must be continuously measured to ensure a predictable flow of qualified opportunities into new client relationships.

---

# Principle

A healthy pipeline produces predictable growth.

The objective is not to have the largest pipeline.

The objective is to have the right prospects moving through the right stages at the right pace.

---

# Why It Matters

Without a measurable pipeline:

- Revenue forecasting becomes unreliable.
- Bottlenecks remain hidden.
- Follow-up becomes inconsistent.
- High-value opportunities are lost.
- Sales activity becomes reactive.
- Growth depends on luck rather than process.

The pipeline provides a real-time view of the future health of the business.

---

# Standard

Every prospect must exist within a defined pipeline stage.

Every movement between stages must be documented.

Pipeline metrics must measure both:

- Quantity
- Quality

A larger pipeline does not necessarily indicate a healthier acquisition system.

---

# Pipeline Stages

The Client Acquisition System recognizes the following standard stages:

1. Identified Prospect
2. Research Completed
3. Initial Contact
4. Active Conversation
5. Discovery Scheduled
6. Discovery Completed
7. Recommendation Presented
8. Proposal Delivered
9. Negotiation
10. Client Won
11. Opportunity Lost
12. Future Opportunity

Every prospect must occupy one—and only one—active stage.

---

# Pipeline Volume Metrics

Measure:

- Total prospects
- Active prospects
- Qualified prospects
- New prospects added
- Opportunities removed
- Opportunities won
- Opportunities lost

Pipeline volume should remain consistent over time.

Large fluctuations require investigation.

---

# Stage Distribution Metrics

Measure the number of prospects in each stage.

Healthy pipelines contain opportunities throughout the entire acquisition journey.

An overfilled stage often indicates a bottleneck.

An empty stage indicates insufficient upstream activity.

Balanced distribution creates predictable client acquisition.

---

# Stage Conversion Metrics

Measure movement between stages, including:

- Prospect → Research
- Research → Initial Contact
- Contact → Active Conversation
- Conversation → Discovery
- Discovery → Recommendation
- Recommendation → Proposal
- Proposal → Client
- Proposal → Lost Opportunity

Conversion rates identify where improvements will have the greatest impact.

---

# Pipeline Velocity

Measure:

- Average days in each stage
- Total acquisition cycle length
- Time between follow-up activities
- Proposal turnaround time
- Time to onboarding

Velocity indicates the efficiency of the acquisition process.

Long delays increase the probability of lost opportunities.

---

# Pipeline Value

Measure:

- Estimated revenue
- Monthly recurring revenue potential
- One-time revenue potential
- Weighted pipeline value
- Forecasted close value

Revenue forecasting depends on pipeline quality, not optimism.

---

# Opportunity Quality

Evaluate opportunities using:

- Ideal Client Score
- Growth potential
- Strategic alignment
- Decision-maker access
- Budget readiness
- Timeline readiness
- Service alignment

High-quality opportunities deserve greater attention.

---

# Pipeline Aging

Measure:

- Average opportunity age
- Stagnant opportunities
- Opportunities without recent activity
- Opportunities exceeding expected stage duration

Every inactive opportunity requires a decision:

- Re-engage
- Move forward
- Place in Future Opportunity
- Close as Lost

An aging pipeline hides operational problems.

---

# Activity Metrics

Track:

- Calls completed
- Emails sent
- Meetings held
- Follow-ups completed
- Discovery sessions
- Recommendations delivered
- Proposals presented

Pipeline movement should correlate with consistent activity.

Activity without movement requires investigation.

---

# Pipeline Health Indicators

A healthy pipeline demonstrates:

- Consistent prospect creation
- Regular stage progression
- Balanced stage distribution
- Predictable conversion
- Low stagnation
- High documentation quality
- Reliable revenue forecasting

Pipeline health is measured by movement, not volume.

---

# CRM Requirements

Every opportunity must include:

- Current stage
- Stage entry date
- Last activity
- Next action
- Assigned owner
- Estimated value
- Expected close date
- Decision-maker status
- Opportunity notes

Incomplete CRM records reduce forecasting accuracy.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing the pipeline, ask:

1. Are enough qualified prospects entering the pipeline?
2. Where are opportunities slowing down?
3. Which stage has the lowest conversion?
4. Which opportunities require immediate attention?
5. Which improvements will increase pipeline flow?

Every review should produce measurable operational actions.

---

# Measurement

Track:

- Pipeline growth rate
- Stage conversion rates
- Pipeline velocity
- Opportunity aging
- Forecast accuracy
- Win rate
- Loss rate
- Average deal size
- Revenue generated
- CRM completeness

Metrics should identify trends before they become problems.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Improve pipeline performance through:

- Weekly pipeline reviews
- CRM audits
- Lost opportunity analysis
- Sales activity reviews
- Conversion analysis
- Forecast validation
- Process optimization
- Team coaching

The pipeline should become more predictable with every review cycle.
