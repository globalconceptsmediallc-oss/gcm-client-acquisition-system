# Conversion Metrics

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Purpose:

Establish the standard for measuring conversion performance throughout the Client Acquisition System. Conversion metrics identify how effectively prospects progress from initial identification to becoming long-term clients and reveal where improvements will create the greatest business impact.

---

# Principle

Growth is not determined by activity alone.

Growth is determined by how efficiently activity becomes opportunity, opportunity becomes clients, and clients become long-term business relationships.

Every conversion rate represents the effectiveness of the system.

---

# Why It Matters

High activity with poor conversion produces wasted effort.

Low activity with high conversion limits growth potential.

Measuring conversion performance allows Global Concepts Media to:

- Identify operational bottlenecks.
- Improve sales effectiveness.
- Increase forecasting accuracy.
- Reduce client acquisition costs.
- Improve client quality.
- Scale predictable business growth.

Conversion metrics transform assumptions into measurable evidence.

---

# Standard

Every major stage within the Client Acquisition System must have a measurable conversion rate.

Conversion metrics must be:

- Consistently calculated.
- Objectively measured.
- Historically tracked.
- Reviewed regularly.
- Used to improve the acquisition system.

The objective is continuous improvement—not perfection.

---

# Core Conversion Stages

The standard conversion sequence is:

1. Prospect Identified
2. Qualified Prospect
3. Initial Contact
4. Active Conversation
5. Discovery Call Scheduled
6. Discovery Call Completed
7. Recommendation Presented
8. Proposal Delivered
9. Proposal Accepted
10. Client Onboarded

Every transition between stages should be measurable.

---

# Primary Conversion Metrics

## Prospect Qualification Rate

Measures:

Qualified Prospects ÷ Total Prospects Identified

Purpose:

Evaluate prospect targeting quality.

---

## Contact Response Rate

Measures:

Prospects Responding ÷ Initial Contacts Made

Purpose:

Measure outreach effectiveness.

---

## Conversation Rate

Measures:

Active Conversations ÷ Responses Received

Purpose:

Evaluate communication quality and engagement.

---

## Discovery Scheduling Rate

Measures:

Discovery Calls Scheduled ÷ Active Conversations

Purpose:

Measure the ability to move conversations toward meaningful business discussions.

---

## Discovery Completion Rate

Measures:

Completed Discovery Calls ÷ Discovery Calls Scheduled

Purpose:

Evaluate commitment and scheduling effectiveness.

---

## Recommendation Rate

Measures:

Recommendations Presented ÷ Completed Discovery Calls

Purpose:

Ensure every qualified discovery produces meaningful business guidance.

---

## Proposal Rate

Measures:

Proposals Delivered ÷ Recommendations Presented

Purpose:

Measure how often recommendations advance into formal business opportunities.

---

## Proposal Acceptance Rate

Measures:

Accepted Proposals ÷ Proposals Delivered

Purpose:

Evaluate proposal quality, pricing alignment, and client confidence.

---

## Client Acquisition Rate

Measures:

New Clients ÷ Qualified Prospects

Purpose:

Measure overall acquisition effectiveness.

---

## Referral Conversion Rate

Measures:

New Clients from Referrals ÷ Referral Opportunities

Purpose:

Evaluate referral quality and relationship strength.

---

# Revenue Conversion Metrics

Track:

- Revenue per prospect.
- Revenue per qualified prospect.
- Revenue per proposal.
- Revenue per discovery call.
- Revenue per referral.
- Average client value.
- Monthly recurring revenue conversion.
- One-time project conversion.

Revenue validates operational performance.

---

# Quality Conversion Metrics

Measure:

- Ideal client conversion rate.
- Strategic fit conversion.
- Client retention after onboarding.
- First 90-day success rate.
- Client satisfaction after acquisition.

Acquiring the wrong client is not a successful conversion.

---

# Stage Loss Analysis

Measure where opportunities are lost.

Examples include:

- Research failures.
- No response.
- Lost after discovery.
- Proposal declined.
- Budget limitations.
- Timing issues.
- Competitive selection.
- Internal business changes.

Every lost opportunity is an opportunity to improve the system.

---

# Benchmark Analysis

Compare conversion performance:

- Week over week.
- Month over month.
- Quarter over quarter.
- Year over year.

Historical trends provide more value than isolated numbers.

Improvement should be measured against previous performance.

---

# CRM Requirements

Every conversion must be supported by documented CRM activity.

Required records include:

- Stage history.
- Dates.
- Responsible owner.
- Supporting notes.
- Outcome.
- Lost opportunity reason when applicable.

Accurate conversion metrics depend upon accurate documentation.

---

# Decision Framework

When reviewing conversion performance, ask:

1. Which conversion rate improved?
2. Which conversion rate declined?
3. Where is the largest loss occurring?
4. What operational change could improve this stage?
5. How will improvement be measured?

Every conversion review should result in an actionable improvement.

---

Success is measured by both efficiency and client quality.
